### Mastering the Stage 2 Uptrend: The Minervini Trend Template

##### 1\. The Foundation: Understanding the Four Stages of a Stock’s Life Cycle

In the rigorous world of professional trading, stock prices do not move in random, chaotic patterns. They follow a process defined as  **"Stock Price Maturation."**  As a professional, you must understand that a stock moves through a distinct life cycle consisting of four specific phases. To achieve "Superperformance"—returns that far exceed the market average—you must identify exactly where a stock sits in this cycle.**The 4 Stages of Price Action**| Stage Number | Phase Name | Core Characteristic | Action for the Trader || \------ | \------ | \------ | \------ || **Stage 1** | Neglect | Consolidation: Sideways movement, lack of institutional interest. | **Avoid**  (Dead money) || **Stage 2** | Advancing | Accumulation: Strong demand, institutional buying, higher highs. | **Buy / Hold** || **Stage 3** | Topping | Distribution: Volatility increases, smart money exits, erratic swings. | **Sell / Take Profit** || **Stage 4** | Declining | Lower Lows: Lack of institutional support, downward spiral. | **Avoid**  (High risk) |  
**The Law of Inertia and Probability Convergence**  Superperformance occurs exclusively in  **Stage 2** . The non-negotiable requirement is to align your capital with the "Law of Inertia": a stock at rest (Stage 1\) tends to stay at rest, while a stock in motion (Stage 2\) tends to stay in motion. While beginners fruitlessly "bottom fish" in Stage 1, the seasoned trader waits for  **Probability Convergence** —the specific point where technicals and fundamentals align—confirming that the "path of least resistance" is upward.*Once you have identified the stage, you must apply a rigorous technical filter to confirm that the trend is professional-grade and sustainable.*

##### 2\. The Non-Negotiable Filter: The 8 Criteria of the Trend Template

The Trend Template is the "gold standard" for identifying a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend. Before a stock is even considered for your portfolio, it must pass these eight technical hurdles.

1. **Price \> 150-day & 200-day MA:**  The current price must be trading above both its 150-day and 200-day moving average lines.  
2. **150-day MA \> 200-day MA:**  The shorter-term 150-day average must be physically above the 200-day average.  
3. **Trending 200-day MA:**  The 200-day moving average line must be trending upward for at least one month, though  **preferably 4–5 months minimum**  in most cases.  
4. **The 50-day MA Multi-Filter:**  The 50-day moving average must be above both the 150-day and 200-day moving averages.  
5. **Price \> 50-day MA:**  The current price must be trading above the 50-day moving average.  
6. **Distance from Low:**  The current stock price must be  **at least 30% above its 52-week low** .  
7. **Proximity to High:**  The current stock price must be  **within 25% of its 52-week high** .  
8. **Relative Strength (RS):**  The stock should have a high Relative Strength rating of  **70 or higher** , though the elite performers typically reside in the  **80s or 90s** .**Note on Relative Strength:**  While the 8th rule is often omitted from automated TradingView scripts due to the lack of proprietary  *Investor’s Business Daily*  data, it remains a vital conceptual component. It signifies that the stock is outperforming the vast majority of the market.*Clearing these technical hurdles is only the first step; as a professional, you must now avoid the psychological traps of "valuation."*

##### 3\. Debunking the P/E Ratio Myth: Value vs. Performance

A common mistake for novices is "bottom fishing" for stocks with low Price/Earnings (P/E) ratios because they look "cheap." In high-performance trading, "cheap" is often a warning sign of a Stage 4 decline. This leads to  **Broken Leader Syndrome** , where beginners buy a formerly great stock because they "missed the original move" and now incorrectly perceive the price collapse as a bargain.**Cheap Trap vs. Superperformer**| Feature | Broken Leaders (The Trap) | High-Performance Leaders || \------ | \------ | \------ || **Price Stage** | Stage 4 (Declining) | Stage 2 (Advancing) || **P/E Ratio** | Falling or at 10-year lows (e.g., AIG) | Expanding (High or rising) || **Market Perception** | "Bargain" | "Expensive" || **Analyst View** | Discounting future failure | Struggling to model high growth |  
**The Ferrari vs. Hyundai Analogy**  High-performance leaders command high P/E multiples for the same reason a  **Ferrari costs more than a Hyundai** : superior engineering and performance.  **Yahoo\!**  traded at a  **938x P/E**  before advancing 7,800%, and  **Taser**  traded at a  **200x P/E**  before a 300% gain.Furthermore, you must understand the P/E paradox: during a stock's most explosive move, the P/E may actually  *drop*  despite the price rising. As seen in the case of  **CKE Restaurants** , the P/E fell from 55 to 47 during a 412% advance because earnings grew even faster than the share price. A "high" price is always relative to the massive growth potential.*Once you have the trend and the story, you must master the "art" of the entry point.*

##### 4\. From Identification to Action: The VCP and the Pivot Point

The Trend Template identifies the trend, but the  **Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)**  identifies the timing. The VCP is the "rest period" where the stock digests its gains.

* **Reduction in Price Volatility:**  The price swings must get smaller and "tighter" as the pattern moves from the  **greater volatility on the left side of the base to the lesser volatility on the right side.**  
* **Reduction in Volume:**  Trading activity must "dry up" on the right side, signifying that supply has been exhausted.  
* **The Pivot Point:**  This is the final, tightest contraction. When the stock breaks above this point on  **heavy volume** , it is the optimal time to buy.**Getting Started: Using the Trend Template in TradingView**  
1. Open a chart and click the  **"Pine Editor"**  tab at the bottom.  
2. Clear the existing code and paste the Minervini Trend Template script.  
3. Click  **"Add to Chart."**  The  **background will turn green**  to visually indicate that all 8 criteria have been met.  
4. **Manual Inspection:**  Once the script flags a stock (green background), you must manually inspect the chart for the "left-to-right" VCP tightening and the Pivot Point.*Technical filters provide the science, but your manual inspection of the VCP is the art of a gifted trader.*

##### 5\. Summary Checklist for the Aspiring Wizard

To achieve superperformance, discipline is more important than the rules themselves. Adhere to this "Wizard's Rulebook":

*   **Verify Stage 2:**  Does the stock meet all 8 Trend Template criteria (including the 4–5 month preference for the 200-day MA)?  
*   **Ignore the "Cheap Trap":**  Avoid the Broken Leader Syndrome. Never buy a stock simply because it is "down 70%."  
*   **Identify the Catalyst:**  Conduct detective work to find the driver—be it  **FDA approval, a new multi-million dollar contract, or a new visionary CEO.**  
*   **Wait for the VCP:**  Confirm the progressive reduction in price volatility from the left side of the base to the right.  
*   **Execute at the Pivot:**  Buy only when the price breaks the pivot point on high volume.**The Absolute Cost of Success**  Success in the market requires sacrifice and a "risk-first" mentality. You must prioritize your strategy over your ego. As Mark Minervini emphasizes, the difference between interest and commitment is the  **will not to give up.**  Knowledge can be acquired, but the professional-grade discipline to follow these rules is what creates a market wizard.
