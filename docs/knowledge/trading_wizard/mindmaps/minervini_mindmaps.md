## 3. The 4-Stage Stock Cycle
Minervini emphasizes only trading in Stage 2. Here is what defines each stage.

```mermaid
mindmap
  root((4_Stage Cycle))
    Stage 1 Neglect
      Sideways Movement
      Low Volume
      Price oscillates around 200 SMA
      No institutional interest yet
    Stage 2 Advancing
      Price above 200 SMA
      200 SMA trending upward
      Higher Highs and Higher Lows
      Volume expands on up days
      Volume contracts on down days
      THE ONLY STAGE TO BUY
    Stage 3 Topping
      High Volatility
      Price breaks below 50 SMA frequently
      Heavy volume on down days
      Earnings growth slows
      Distribution by institutions
    Stage 4 Declining
      Price below 200 SMA
      200 SMA trending downward
      Lower Highs and Lower Lows
      Negative earnings surprises
```

## 4. Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)
The VCP is Minervini's signature technical setup. It represents the absorption of supply.

```mermaid
mindmap
  root((VCP Setup))
    Definition
      Volatility Contraction Pattern
      Supply Absorption
      Constructive Price Consolidation
    Characteristics
      Decreasing Price Volatility
        Left side loose
        Right side tight
      Contraction Cycles
        2 to 6 contractions
        e.g. 25 percent down to 10 percent to 4 percent
      Volume Dry_up
        Volume heavily dries up on the right side
        Sign of zero supply
    The Buy Point
      Pivot Point Breakout
      Increase in Volume
      Crossing the Line of Least Resistance
    Variations
      Cup and Handle
      Double Bottom
      Flat Base
      High Tight Flag
```

## 5. Risk Management & Mindset
Without risk management, the strategy fails. This is the psychological and mathematical foundation.

```mermaid
mindmap
  root((Risk Management))
    Mathematical Reality
      Losses work geometrically against you
      50 percent loss requires 100 percent gain
      Keep losses small and manageable
    The Stop Loss
      Always use a hard stop
      Maximum 10 percent loss
      Average loss should be 5 to 6 percent
      Move stop to break_even when profitable
    Position Sizing
      Never risk more than 1_2 percent of total equity per trade
      Optimal sizing 12_25 percent per position
      Concentrate don't over_diversify
    Psychology
      Trade without Ego
      Hope is not a strategy
      Accept losses as business expenses
      Discipline over conviction
```

## 6. Earnings Reports & Fundamentals (Code 33)
Minervini heavily focuses on specific fundamental metrics found in Earnings Reports. "Code 33" represents the holy trinity of fundamental acceleration.

```mermaid
mindmap
  root((Earnings Reports
  Fundamentals))
    Earnings Surprises
      Beat Wall Street Estimates
      Positive revisions by analysts
      Look for big percentage beats
    Earnings Acceleration
      Quarter over quarter EPS growth
      Growth rate must be accelerating
      e.g. 15 percent to 25 percent to 40 percent
    Revenue Growth
      Must be backed by sales growth
      Cost cutting is not sustainable
      Look for double digit sales growth
    Margin Expansion
      Operating Margins increasing
      Net Margins increasing
      Shows pricing power and efficiency
    Code 33
      Accelerating Earnings
      Accelerating Sales
      Expanding Margins
    Annual vs Quarterly
      Current quarter is most important
      Annual EPS must show consistent uptrend
      Breakout years
```

## 7. Stock Categorization Details
Knowing what category a stock belongs to dictates your expectations and holding period.

```mermaid
mindmap
  root((Stock Categories))
    Market Leaders
      Top 10 to 20 percent of companies
      Fastest earnings growth
      Best price action in the group
      This is the primary target
    Top Competitors
      Strong peers in the same industry
      Moves in sympathy with the Leader
      Can become the new leader
    Institutional Favorites
      Quality growth stocks
      Steady predictable earnings
      Cookie Cutter business models
      e.g. McDonald's or Walmart in their prime
    Turnaround Situations
      Recovering from depressed earnings
      New management or product
      Requires at least 1 or 2 strong quarters
      High risk high reward
    Cyclical Stocks
      Earnings tied to economic cycles
      e.g. Autos Commodities Semiconductors
      Must buy when P E is HIGH
      Sell when P E is LOW
    Laggards
      Avoid at all costs
      Cheap for a reason
      Weak relative strength
```
